📊 Volume Profile Shapes read the day's character

The shape of the profile tells you what kind of day it is — and therefore which strategy is even valid. Mean-reversion only works on balanced (D) shapes; on trend/P/b shapes the value-area edge breaks instead of holding. Identify the shape first, then pick the setup.

Rule of thumb: Ask "is the market balancing (rotating around a fair price) or trending (seeking a new one)?" Balanced → fade the edges to POC. Trending → trade with the move, never fade it. Double distribution → trade the middle LVN as a pivot.
Honest caveat (from the research, not hype): these tools don't predict — they locate asymmetric spots (tight stop, big target, stacked confluence). Common myths to drop: "every FVG must fill" (false — many never do in strong trends), "institutions leave orders in gaps" (unprovable). Focus on what's observable: sweep → displacement → structure break → confirmation. Edge comes from repeating an A+ setup 100s of times with strict risk — which is why the Journal & Leaderboard matter more than any indicator.

Edge Score: Expectancy × √(min(N,100)) × confidence — rewards positive expectancy, scales with sample size, penalizes thin samples. Scale up proven & positive · Keep testing too few · Cut / fix negative.
